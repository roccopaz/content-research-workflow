# SOP — Tech Motivates Trend-to-Content Pipeline

Last updated: 2026-03-17
Owner: Roni ⚡ (orchestration)
Research owner: Robo 🤖
Content owner: Star ⭐

## Purpose

Create a repeatable operating system for Tech Motivates across TikTok and YouTube Shorts.

This SOP turns:
- trend signals
- viral short-form patterns
- famous speeches
- quotes and sayings
- athlete / rapper / role-model moments

into:
- ranked content opportunities
- ready-to-produce concepts
- hooks, captions, and packaging
- a weekly backlog that can actually get posted

## North Star

Make motivational short-form content that feels current, emotionally strong, and brand-fit.

The channel should emphasize:
- discipline
- resilience
- confidence
- sacrifice
- ambition
- comeback energy
- winner mentality
- inspiring lines from recognizable people

## Role Split

### Robo owns research
Robo is responsible for:
- trend discovery
- pattern detection
- competitor observation
- topic mapping
- opportunity ranking
- weekly/daily trend briefs

Robo's core question:
**What is winning right now that fits Tech Motivates?**

### Star owns content conversion
Star is responsible for:
- turning trend briefs into short-form concepts
- hooks
- scripts / quote framing
- on-screen text
- captions / titles
- edit direction
- backlog management

Star's core question:
**How do we turn this into content people will actually watch and share?**

## Content Pillars

All work should map to one or more of these pillars.

### 1) Athlete Mindset
Examples:
- Kobe, Jordan, LeBron, Ali, Goggins, post-game mindset, training mentality, pain, sacrifice

### 2) Rapper / Artist Ambition
Examples:
- Nipsey Hussle, Jay-Z, 50 Cent, Kanye, Drake, artists speaking on hunger, ownership, growth, struggle

### 3) Famous Speech Moments
Examples:
- Denzel Washington, Eric Thomas, Inky Johnson, Les Brown, Jim Rohn, interview or speech clips with a strong takeaway

### 4) Quote-Driven Inspiration
Examples:
- discipline over motivation
- work in silence
- pressure creates diamonds
- keep going
- self-belief
- purpose

### 5) Timely Cultural Energy
Examples:
- playoffs
- championship runs
- comeback stories
- viral athlete interviews
- culturally relevant moments that can be reframed into motivation

## Workflow Overview

1. Robo scans for trend signals
2. Robo publishes a trend brief
3. Star converts the brief into a content pack
4. Star ranks ideas into immediate / weekly / evergreen
5. Tech or Star selects concepts for production
6. Published results feed back into the next trend cycle

---

## Stage 1 — Robo Daily Trend Pulse

### Goal
Find current signals without overproducing noise.

### Frequency
Daily

### Inputs Robo should check
- YouTube Shorts trends in motivation-adjacent categories
- TikTok motivation / mindset / grind patterns
- athlete / rapper / entrepreneur / speech clips gaining traction
- competitor channels in motivation and inspirational edits
- repeated hook formats
- comment patterns showing desire, aspiration, pain, ambition, comeback, discipline
- relevant current events that can be converted into motivational framing

### Robo output format
**Daily Trend Pulse**
- Date:
- 3 rising themes:
- 3 people/topics worth watching:
- best athlete angle:
- best rapper/artist angle:
- best speech/quote angle:
- oversaturated angle to avoid:
- best immediate content opportunity:
- confidence: low / medium / high

### Daily quality bar
Only surface items that are:
- timely
- emotionally usable
- visually usable
- relevant to Tech Motivates

If nothing strong is found, Robo should say so instead of forcing weak ideas.

---

## Stage 2 — Robo Weekly Trend Memo

### Goal
Produce the strategic weekly input for Star.

### Frequency
Weekly

### Robo weekly deliverable
**Weekly Trend Memo**
- date range covered
- strongest themes this week
- strongest people / topics this week
- strongest hook styles
- strongest editing / pacing styles showing up
- what's fading
- what's oversaturated
- top 10 opportunities for Tech Motivates
- top 3 highest-priority recommendations for next 7 days

### Required scoring for each opportunity
Score 1–5:
- trend strength
- brand fit
- emotional power
- visual potential
- retention potential
- replayability
- production ease

Then assign:
- A-tier = move now
- B-tier = keep in weekly queue
- C-tier = archive / revisit only if needed

---

## Stage 3 — Star Trend-to-Content Conversion

### Goal
Turn Robo's research into actual content concepts.

### Frequency
Daily after Robo pulse, plus weekly batch after weekly memo

### Star input
- Robo Daily Trend Pulse
- Robo Weekly Trend Memo
- recent channel performance notes
- any Tech preferences for voice/style this week

### Star output
**Content Pack**
For each concept include:
- concept name
- pillar
- emotional angle
- source type (athlete / rapper / speech / quote / role model)
- hook option 1
- hook option 2
- on-screen text
- caption
- CTA if needed
- visual direction
- pacing/edit style
- TikTok version note
- YouTube Shorts version note
- confidence score
- production difficulty

### Star rules
Star should optimize for:
- immediate clarity in first 1–2 seconds
- emotional intensity
- quotability
- replay value
- ease of production
- platform-specific packaging

Star should avoid:
- vague fluff
- generic motivational filler
- recycled ideas with no new angle
- content that is trendy but off-brand

---

## Stage 4 — Backlog Management

Star keeps concepts in three buckets:

### Immediate
Use now.
Shelf life: 24–72 hours

### Weekly
Relevant this week but not urgent.
Shelf life: 3–7 days

### Evergreen
Can run anytime.
Examples:
- discipline
- sacrifice
- self-belief
- underdog
- comeback
- lonely work
- pain into progress

Each concept should also carry tags such as:
- athlete
- rapper
- speech
- quote
- grind
- confidence
- comeback
- discipline
- leadership
- underdog

---

## Stage 5 — Production Prep

For approved concepts, Star should prepare a production-ready spec.

### Production spec template
- target platform: TikTok / Shorts / both
- target duration: 12–30s
- opening 0:00–0:02 hook:
- middle message / quote:
- payoff / final line:
- suggested clip type:
- suggested subtitle style:
- emphasized words:
- music mood:
- ending loop idea:

### Timing guideline
Typical structure:
- 0:00–0:02 = hard hook
- 0:02–0:07 = context / emotional setup
- 0:07–0:15 = strongest quote / speech line
- 0:15–0:20 = final punch or replayable loop

## Stage 6 — Performance Feedback Loop

### Goal
Use outcomes to improve future content selection and packaging.

### Track when available
- views
- average watch time
- retention
- rewatches
- likes
- comments
- shares
- saves
- strongest hook type
- strongest source type
- strongest emotional theme

### Feedback usage
Robo uses results to improve future trend filtering.
Star uses results to improve hooks, pacing, captions, and packaging.

Questions to review weekly:
- Are athlete clips outperforming rapper clips?
- Are direct quotes outperforming longer speeches?
- Is raw emotional intensity beating polished inspiration?
- Is TikTok responding better to aggressive hooks than Shorts?
- Which themes are oversaturated and declining?

---

## Platform Adaptation Rules

### TikTok
Bias toward:
- faster emotional entry
- stronger curiosity
- rawer, more native feeling edits
- sharper hooks
- slightly more aggressive packaging

### YouTube Shorts
Bias toward:
- cleaner structure
- clearer visual cohesion
- stronger loop endings
- more evergreen packaging
- titles / framing that still make sense after the trend fades

Same concept can have two variants.
Star should create both when practical.

---

## Approval and Handoff Rules

### Robo → Star handoff must include
- trend/topic
- why it matters now
- expected shelf life
- audience emotion it taps into
- example format references if useful
- recommended priority score

### Star → production handoff must include
- final selected concept
- hook
- text overlay
- caption
- visual guidance
- pacing notes
- priority and deadline

### Escalate to Tech when
- the source is high-risk or questionable
- the topic is trending but off-brand
- multiple strong ideas compete for the same slot
- the idea may need a stronger creative call than the SOP can decide

### Weekly
Robo:
- deliver Weekly Trend Memo

Star:

- prepare 10–20 ranked concepts
- identify top 5 priority posts

## Success Criteria

### Robo is succeeding if
- Star always has fresh, relevant inputs
- weak/noisy trends are filtered out
- the opportunity list feels current, not random

### Star is succeeding if
- concepts are easy to produce
- hooks are strong
- packaging is platform-aware
- there is always a usable backlog
- ideas feel emotionally sharp and brand-fit

### System is succeeding if
- content moves faster from idea to post
- the backlog stays fresh
- more posts are trend-aligned without feeling copycat
- performance insights improve future content choices
